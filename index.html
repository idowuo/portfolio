The diagnosis was organoaxial gastric rotation of the stomach. Normally it's shaped like a J, but when Peter had an episode, it would flip 180 degrees, with the crook of the J facing down instead of up. It happened when his stomach was full, a helluva way to digest a meal. Luckily it always flipped back or he would have been "severely compromised," Haddad says, or worse. Relieved that it could be corrected by surgery, Simonetti had her humor back. When she first met the surgeon, Dr. Olajire Idowu, she says her opening comment was, "This isn't your first day on the job, is it?" It's a running gag and even Idowu got a laugh out of it. He's 60, and is the chair of the department of surgery at Children's. The surgery took about 90 minutes. The problem was a birth defect. The stomach is normally joined by ligaments to the spleen, diaphragm, liver and colon. The ligaments were there, but they were "loose like a rubber band," Idowu says, and wouldn't hold the stomach in place. The repair was a relatively simple operation to sew the stomach to the interior aspect of the upper abdominal wall, using permanent sutures. Temperature Conversion

This Ruby program will prompt the user for a temperature in degrees Celsius and let the user know what the corresponding temperature is in Fahrenheit. 

            
            puts "Enter degrees in Celcius"
            cel = gets.chomp
            fah = cel.to_f * 1.8 + 32
            puts fah
            val = "The temperature is #{fah} degrees Fahrenheit"
            puts val

Ordinal Challenge

This ruby program will convert a plain number to the ordinal of the number. So for example, if the user enters 2, it will display 2nd, if the user enters 3, it will display 3rd, etc. 


         puts "Enter a number"
        number = gets.chomp.to_i

        if number == 11
           puts "#{number}th"
        elsif number == 12
           puts "#{number}th"   
        elsif number == 13
           puts "#{number}th" 
        elsif number.digits.first == 1
           puts "#{number}st"
        elsif number.digits. first == 2
           puts "#{number}nd"
        elsif number.digits.first == 3
           puts "#{number}rd"
        else
           puts "#{number}th"
        end
